About Me

I am a graduate student in Computational Communication at Jinan University. My research focuses on deepfake-related misinformation, trust mechanisms, and accessible design for vulnerable populations such as older adults and individuals with intellectual disabilities. I combine qualitative coding, machine learning, and agent-based modeling to explore social risks and digital inclusion.

Selected Papers

  • Deepfake Fraud and the Risk Society: Understanding the manipulation of trust through AI-generated media. Information, Communication & Society (under review). [PDF]
  • Modeling Elderly Trust and Deception: An agent-based simulation integrating GPT and Bayesian game theory. IEEE MiTA 2025. [PDF]
  • Bridging Information Inequality: Designing an accessible social media platform for individuals with intellectual disabilities. [PDF]

Research Projects

  • Deepfake Discourse Dynamics (2017–2025): Collected 88k+ Weibo posts to analyze topic evolution, sentiment, and forecasting using BERTopic and time-series models.
  • Elder Scam Simulation System: Built an agent-based framework integrating GPT-based persona modeling and intervention evaluation for elderly protection research.
  • Inclusive Information Platform: Designed and prototyped an accessible media system combining semantic simplification and participatory design with individuals with intellectual disabilities.
